# Professional Development Roadmap Builder

## Overview

Combines **career development**, **learning mindset**, **personal leadership**, **productivity-performance**, and **communication-influence** to produce a complete, values-aligned professional development roadmap with clear priorities, skill-building plans, and a realistic implementation architecture.

## When to Use

- Professional asks: "build me a development plan", "how should I grow professionally?", "what should I focus on for the next year?"
- Preparing for a performance review or development conversation
- At a career inflection point: new role, promotion target, career transition
- Annual planning: designing the year's professional growth investments

## Required Inputs

1. **Professional profile** — current role, industry, years of experience, career stage
2. **Career ambition** — where do you want to be in 3 years? What does career success look like to you?
3. **Strengths** — what do colleagues consistently recognise you for? What do you do that feels effortless?
4. **Development gaps** — what is currently limiting your impact or advancement? What feedback have you received?
5. **Values** — what matters most to you in work? (autonomy, impact, learning, relationships, security, creativity, etc.)
6. **Constraints** — available time per week for development, budget for courses/coaching, organisational context

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Career Capital Assessment (Career Development lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_career-development.md`

- Map current career capital: what skills does this professional have that are rare and valuable?
- Identify career anchors (Schein): which of the 8 career anchors dominate their orientation?
- Assess career trajectory: is the current path building toward the stated 3-year ambition?
- Prioritise development investments using: strategic value × skill gap × development speed

### Step 2 — Values and Purpose Alignment Check
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_purpose-values.md`

- Verify the career ambition is genuinely values-aligned — not externally imposed
- Run a quick values-behaviour audit: what proportion of current work time is spent on values-aligned activities?
- Identify if any development goal is driven by "should" vs. genuine motivation
- Flag if purpose clarity work is needed before skill development planning makes sense

### Step 3 — Learning Architecture Design (Learning Mindset lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_learning-mindset.md`

For the top 2-3 development priorities:
- Specify the current skill stage (beginner / intermediate / advanced) and the target
- Design deliberate practice structures: what are the stretch goals, feedback mechanisms, and review cycles?
- Recommend specific learning resources (format and type, not specific products)
- Build a spaced repetition and retrieval practice approach for knowledge-heavy areas
- Identify the training edge for each skill: the specific sub-component where focused practice will produce the greatest return

### Step 4 — Leadership and Communication Growth Plan
Read agents: `agents/finta_holistic_personal-leadership.md`, `agents/finta_holistic_communication-influence.md`

- Identify the leadership development priority: self-awareness, influence, decision-making, communication, or team effectiveness
- Design specific leadership development experiments for the next quarter
- Build a communication skills development focus: which channel (written / verbal / presenting / listening) needs most attention?
- Identify 3 relationships in the professional's current context to deepen as part of leadership development

### Step 5 — Productivity Architecture
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_productivity-performance.md`

- Design the weekly protected learning time: how many hours, when, and what protection mechanisms?
- Identify the top 2 productivity constraints that will prevent development plan execution
- Build a minimum viable deep work schedule that fits the professional's actual role demands
- Design a review cadence: weekly development review + monthly progress assessment

## Output Quality Standards

- Goals must connect to explicit career capital logic — not generic self-improvement
- Every goal must have a specific learning method, not just an intention
- The weekly time allocation must be realistic — development plans that require 20 hours/week of additional work will not be executed
- Values alignment must be verified before committing to the plan — a misaligned development plan is wasted energy
- The plan must include a review mechanism — development plans without review and recalibration drift
