# Purpose and Career Alignment Process

## Overview

Combines **purpose-values**, **career-development**, **personal-leadership**, and **emotional-intelligence** to guide professionals through a rigorous discovery process that uncovers authentic purpose, tests values alignment, and translates insight into concrete career decisions and actions.

## When to Use

- Professional says: "I don't know why I'm doing this anymore", "I've achieved what I set out to but don't feel fulfilled", "I need to find what I'm meant to do"
- Considering a major career pivot or life redesign
- Post-achievement emptiness: reached the goal, summit feels wrong
- Wanting to move from a career designed by external expectations to one designed by internal values

## Required Inputs

1. **Current situation** — role, industry, years in this career path
2. **Trigger** — what brought them to this question now?
3. **Life snapshot** — brief description of life outside work (relationships, interests, health, commitments)
4. **Peak experiences** — 3 moments in any domain (work or life) of deep satisfaction, meaning, or flow
5. **Violation moments** — 2-3 moments of deep discomfort, anger, or shame — when values were violated
6. **Constraints** — financial reality, family commitments, geographic constraints

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Values Excavation (Purpose-Values lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_purpose-values.md`

Conduct structured values excavation:

**Peak experience analysis**: for each of the 3 peak moments provided —
- What were you doing specifically?
- What made it meaningful rather than just pleasant?
- What values were being expressed in that moment?

**Violation inventory**: for each violation moment —
- What specifically felt wrong?
- What value was being compromised?
- How did you respond — and what does that reveal?

**Narrative pattern**: across all 5-6 experiences, what themes emerge?
- What values appear consistently across peak and violation experiences?
- What is the underlying orientation that connects them?

**Values shortlist**: identify the 3-5 core values — not from a list of words, but emergent from the stories.

### Step 2 — Current Alignment Audit (Career Development lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_career-development.md`

Assess current career against the discovered values:
- Time audit: what proportion of actual working time is spent on values-aligned activities?
- Energy audit: what activities energise vs. deplete? Does this match the values profile?
- Career capital assessment: what rare and valuable skills has this person built? Are these skills in service of their values?
- Identify the specific gap between current role and values-aligned work

### Step 3 — Identity and Leadership Self-Concept (Personal Leadership lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_personal-leadership.md`

- Examine the professional identity: who have they become through this career?
- Identify which parts of their professional identity are authentic vs. performed
- Explore: what kind of leader / contributor / creator do they want to be?
- Design the leadership identity they want to inhabit in the next chapter

### Step 4 — Emotional Processing (Emotional Intelligence lens)
Read agent: `agents/finta_holistic_emotional-intelligence.md`

- Acknowledge the emotional complexity of this conversation: grief for paths not taken, anxiety about change, excitement about possibility
- Help the professional name and honour emotions rather than bypassing them toward premature decision-making
- Assess: is this a genuine values-alignment crisis or is it primarily an emotion regulation challenge (burnout, depression, stress) that would resolve with recovery?

### Step 5 — Ikigai Mapping

Map the four Ikigai dimensions using what has been discovered:

```
IKIGAI MAP

What you love (values + peak experiences):
→ [emerging themes]

What you're good at (career capital + skills):
→ [rare/valuable skills inventory]

What the world needs (genuine contribution):
→ [where your skills + values intersect with real human need]

What you can be paid for (economic viability):
→ [market assessment of where the above intersection has economic value]

CURRENT ALIGNMENT
Intersection exists: [Y/N / partial]
Missing dimension: [which quadrant is weakest?]
Development needed: [what needs to be built to reach the intersection?]
```

### Step 6 — Career Design Options

Generate 3 career design scenarios (Burnett & Evans — Designing Your Life):

```
CAREER DESIGN OPTIONS

Option A — Evolution (low disruption)
Description: [transform current trajectory toward alignment]
Required changes: [specific adjustments to role/focus/relationships]
Timeline to alignment: [realistic estimate]
Risk: [what could go wrong]
Test: [small experiment to validate this path]

Option B — Pivot (moderate disruption)
Description: [adjacent move leveraging existing career capital]
Career capital bridge: [which existing skills transfer]
New skills required: [specific gaps]
Timeline to viability: [realistic]
Risk: [what could go wrong]
Test: [small experiment to validate this path]

Option C — Reinvention (high disruption)
Description: [significant change in direction]
What you keep: [transferable capital]
What you build: [new skills/relationships/context]
Financial runway required: [honest assessment]
Risk: [what could go wrong]
Test: [small experiment to validate this path]
```

### Step 7 — Action Architecture

Design the first 90 days of purposeful action:

```
PURPOSE AND CAREER ALIGNMENT PLAN

Discovered core values: [3-5]
Purpose working hypothesis: [draft statement — to be tested, not final]

Selected direction: [Option A/B/C or hybrid]

90-Day Action Plan
Month 1 — Insight deepening:
  [2-3 small experiments to test the hypothesis]
  [1-2 conversations with people living the target direction]

Month 2 — Capability building:
  [first skill development actions]
  [network expansion toward new context]

Month 3 — Commitment evaluation:
  [review of experiments + insights]
  [decision point: continue, adjust, or choose alternative option]

Review: [what question will you answer in 90 days?]
```

## Output Quality Standards

- Values must be excavated from real experiences, not selected from a list
- The Ikigai map must include an honest economic viability assessment — ignoring financial reality produces beautiful plans that cannot be executed
- Career options must include test experiments — purpose clarity without action testing produces paralysis
- Emotional processing must be respected — do not rush to decision before the person is ready
- The purpose statement is a hypothesis, not a final answer — it will be refined through living, not through more reflection
